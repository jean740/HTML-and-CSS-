Scottish Castles

Here is some information about each castle.


Eilean Donan Castle is thought to be one of the most photographed monuments in Scotland, U.K. It was built in the 13th century, it would become the strong hold for the MacKenzie Clan. In modern time it has been featured in a number of Hollywood films.(To name a few:Highlander, The World is Not Enough and Elizabeth: The golden Age.)

Stirling Castle is one of the largest and most significant castles in Scotland, U.K. It sits strategically on Castle Hill (Castle Hiill is part of a 350 million-year-old rockformation). The castle has been sieged at least eight times, including the unsuccessful one by Bonnie Prince Charlie.

Edinburgh Castle was built on an extinct volcano and this location makes for stunning views of the city. It has been used for a lot of key monments in Scotland's history, including the Wars of Independence to the Jacobite up rising.

Dunnottar Castle was built on a 160ft. rockformation over looking the North Sea. It was once the formidable fortress of the Earls Marischal and also known as the hiding place of the Scottish Crown Jewels.

Culzean Castle stands on the clifftop with stunning views of the Firth of Clyde and the Isle of Arran. It was built in the 18th century, it has some of the most beautiful architecture, lovely gardens and beach front.

Dunrobin Castle has a fairytale architecture look to it, that resembles the French Chateau. It dates back to the 14th century and was home to the Earls and Dukes of Sutherland.
